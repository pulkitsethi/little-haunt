# Prep: Scratch before Session 1

Do this alone (or with a parent) **before** you sit down with her. Setup friction kills the fun.

## Checklist

- [ ] Open [scratch.mit.edu](https://scratch.mit.edu) in Chrome, Edge, or Firefox (not an old tablet browser if you can help it)
- [ ] Create a Scratch account with a **parent email** (she can pick the username — spooky names welcome)
- [ ] Confirm the email if Scratch asks
- [ ] Click **Create** once so the editor loads and works offline-ish after first load
- [ ] Test that sound works (Scratch needs permission sometimes — allow it)
- [ ] Bookmark the editor: [scratch.mit.edu/projects/editor](https://scratch.mit.edu/projects/editor/)
- [ ] Optional: join with headphones ready for spooky sounds later

## Account tips

- Username ideas if she’s stuck: `Haunted[HerName]`, `GhostKitty12`, `LittleHaunt`
- Password: parent manages it; write it somewhere safe
- Keep the project **private** until she’s ready to share (default is fine)

## What you need on the table

- Laptop or Chromebook with a trackpad or mouse (mouse is easier for blocks)
- 30–45 minutes without rushing
- This kit open on your phone, or printed Session 1 page

## You’re ready when…

You can open the Scratch editor in under 30 seconds and she can start drawing immediately — no account screens during “game time.”
