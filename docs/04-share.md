# Share the spooky game

Sharing is half the fun. Keep it light and warn about mild scares.

## Before anyone plays

Say out loud:

> “It’s a little spooky — Halloween scary, not nightmare scary. Ready?”

If a younger sibling is watching, offer to skip the jump-scare sound or turn volume down.

## Share from Scratch (her real project)

1. Open the project → top right **Share** (when she’s proud enough)
2. Copy the project link
3. Send to family text/email:  
   `I made a horror game! [link] — press the green flag. Arrow keys to move.`
4. Or keep it unshared and play live on your laptop at the dinner table (often better for age 10)

**My Stuff:** [scratch.mit.edu/mystuff](https://scratch.mit.edu/mystuff) — find the project anytime.

## Share this kit’s demo (instant audience)

If Scratch isn’t shared yet, open the kit’s playable demo:

- Open `play/index.html` in a browser (double-click, or drag into Chrome)
- Or from this folder: see README for a one-line local server

Same warning: mild spooky chase game, arrow keys, reach the exit.

## After each milestone

| Milestone | What to share |
| --- | --- |
| End of Session 1 | “Watch me walk through my haunted hallway!” |
| End of Session 2 | “Don’t let the ghost get me!” / “Don’t open the closet!” |
| End of Session 3 | Full game — family takes turns; she coaches them |

Record a 20-second phone video of her playing if grandparents are far away.

## Pride rules

- She demos. You don’t narrate over her unless she asks.
- If something breaks during the demo, laugh and say “live bug — we’ll fix it next time.”
- Never say “it’s just a simple Scratch game.” Call it **her horror game**.

## Optional: remix culture later

When she wants a second game, show Scratch’s **Remix** on projects she likes — still her art and her scares first.
