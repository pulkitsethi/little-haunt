Little HauntMake a spooky game she’ll own.

A short mentoring kit for building a first horror game in Scratch — she creates, you unstick the blocks.

Start with setup Try the hallway demo

Your role

You are co-pilot, not engineer. After each session she should say “I made that scary game.”

She owns

Story, monster, art, what’s scary, and the mouse. Cute and janky is perfect.

You own

Account setup, finding the right Scratch block, and calm help when she’s stuck.

Prep Scratch first

Do this alone before she sits down — setup screens kill the magic.

  • Open scratch.mit.edu and create an account with a parent email
  • Confirm she can pick a spooky username
  • Click Create once so the editor loads
  • Allow sound; bookmark the editor
  • Have 30–45 quiet minutes ready

Sessions

One composition at a time: tiny haunted game, one mechanic, playable every time you stop.

  1. Session 1

    Name it, draw it, move

    Game title, dark backdrop, hero walks with arrow keys. Show someone before you quit.

    Open Session 1 guide
  2. Sessions 2–3

    One scare, then win or lose

    Chase or jump scare first. Then exit = escaped, monster touch = caught, plus a sound.

    Open Sessions 2–3 guide
  3. Anytime

    Block cheat sheet

    Motion, looks, broadcast, touching — the horror blocks on one page.

    Open cheat sheet

Easy Scratch scares

These feel advanced; they’re mostly costumes, sounds, and broadcasts.

Chase

point towards hero + move 3 steps forever.

Jump scare

Touch door → broadcast → show big costume + scream.

Flicker

Rapid hide/show or swap backdrops.

Endings

Backdrops: YOU ESCAPED vs IT GOT YOU.

What to say

  • Opening“What’s scary in your game? Who are we? What are we running from?”
  • During“What should happen when you touch the door?”
  • Stuck“Want me to find the block, or want a hint?”
  • Closing“Want to scare Mom/Dad/your brother with what you made?”
